Chess Remix

How To Play

Here we describe the basic movement requirements for all the pieces in greater detail-
Pawns: Normally, you would advance one space. To kill, move one place diagonally. On the first move, you may move two spaces forward. However, it is possible that you will be slain. After this move, En Passant attacks the space behind the pawn. By reaching the other side of the board, it can be swapped for any piece save a King.
Bishops: Any distance can be travelled diagonally. By landing on a spot occupied by an opponent's piece, you can kill them.
Knights: Make a "L" form with your movements (one space one direction, two in a perpendicular direction). Other components may be "overlooked." By landing on a spot occupied by an opponent's piece, you can kill them.
Kings: Horizontally, vertically, or diagonally, move one space. By landing on a spot occupied by an opponent's piece, you can kill them. Under appropriate conditions, a rook may "castle". When the opponent's king cannot escape being captured, the game is ended (i.e. Checkmate). if one player can't advance without jeopardising his or her king (i.e. Stalemate)
Powerups-
Power King: for one turn king can move similar to the queen.
Rook Wall: walls are formed along the selected rooks movement lines till the board boundaries or until another piece is faced. These walls act as barriers and prevent opponent pieces from moving through them for one turn.
Bishop Barrier: barriers are formed around the current player pieces which fall along the selected bishop movement lines.
Paid Promotion: the selected pawn can be promoted to any piece other than the king by paying the required amount.
